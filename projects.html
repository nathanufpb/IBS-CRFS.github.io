Research Projects

Current and completed research projects at IBS-CRFS

Biodiversity Assessment of Atlantic Forest Soils

Status: Ongoing

A comprehensive study of soil fauna diversity in Atlantic Forest remnants, focusing on the impact of forest fragmentation on soil biodiversity.

Duration: 2023-2026

Funding: CNPq

Taxonomy of Neotropical Collembola

Status: Ongoing

Taxonomic revision of springtail (Collembola) species in tropical and subtropical regions, with description of new species.

Duration: 2022-2025

Funding: FAPESP

Soil Fauna and Ecosystem Services

Status: Ongoing

Investigating the role of soil fauna in nutrient cycling and their contribution to ecosystem services in agricultural and natural systems.

Duration: 2023-2027

Funding: CAPES

Impact of Land Use Change on Soil Invertebrates

Status: Completed

Assessed how conversion of native vegetation to agriculture affects soil invertebrate communities and soil functions.

Duration: 2020-2023

Funding: CNPq

Molecular Systematics of Soil Mites

Status: Ongoing

Using molecular tools to resolve phylogenetic relationships and cryptic diversity in soil mite (Acari) groups.

Duration: 2024-2028

Funding: FAPESP

Soil Biodiversity Monitoring Network

Status: Ongoing

Establishing a long-term monitoring network to track changes in soil biodiversity across different ecosystems.

Duration: 2023-2030

Funding: Multiple sources

Collaborative Projects

We actively collaborate with national and international institutions on various research projects. These partnerships enhance our research capacity and contribute to broader understanding of soil biodiversity.

Research Opportunities

We welcome researchers interested in developing collaborative projects. Graduate students and postdoctoral researchers are encouraged to contact us regarding research opportunities at IBS-CRFS.
